Our journey from humble beginnings to industry leadership

Founder's Story

2011
Humble beginnings

Flawless Steel Welding began in 2011 when Victor Garcia, an ironworker facing the fallout of the Great Recession, decided to bet on himself. With just $10,500, a welder, and a pickup truck, Victor started taking on small mobile welding jobs around Denver. One of his first projects—a simple lamp repair near Sloan's Lake—symbolized the determination that would later fuel the company's growth.

2017
Rapid growth

By 2017, word-of-mouth and relentless quality standards had propelled Flawless into a multimillion-dollar operation. The company grew to more than 25 employees, serving commercial clients across Denver, and crossing $3 million in annual revenue. Investments in equipment, shop infrastructure, and early process controls began laying the groundwork for larger projects.

2022
Union partnership

Three years ago, Flawless proudly joined Ironworkers Local 24, strengthening our workforce with union-trained professionals. This milestone not only elevated the quality and safety of our field operations but also ensured sustainable, long-term career paths for our ironworkers. Aligning with the union reinforced our belief that people are the core of every successful project.

2025
Today

Now in our 14th year, Flawless Steel Welding has evolved into a leading AISC-certified fabricator and erector, trusted on complex commercial steel packages throughout Colorado. We are minority-owned, proudly Latino-led, and certified as a small and disadvantaged business enterprise, serving both private and public sector clients. With a workforce of skilled shop fabricators, ironworkers, and project managers, Flawless delivers precision steel solutions while investing in the community we call home.

What Sets Us Apart

Quality & Certification

As one of Denver's few firms certified by the American Institute of Steel Construction (AISC) for both fabrication and erection, Flawless upholds industry-leading standards in quality, safety, and accountability.

Minority-Owned Leadership

Flawless is proudly Latino-owned and nationally recognized for minority-business excellence. Our leadership is active in regional trade groups, ensuring that diversity, integrity, and opportunity are at the heart of our business.

Community Bonds

We don't just build steel; we build relationships. From company-wide barbecues to serving on industry boards, Flawless fosters a culture of trust, belonging, and shared pride.

Why "Flawless"?

The name isn't a marketing gimmick—it's a standard. To us, "Flawless" means:

  • Every weld is held to the highest quality.
  • Every safety briefing is delivered with seriousness and care.
  • Every project is executed with accountability from start to finish.

Safety Training & Certification

Complete all required safety training modules before accessing shop or site areas

Safety is our #1 Priority - All training must be completed before shop/site access

PPE Requirements

Mandatory PPE:

  • Hard Hat (ANSI Z89.1)
  • Safety Glasses (ANSI Z87.1)
  • Steel-Toed Boots (ASTM F2413)
  • High-Visibility Vest
  • Work Gloves

Specialized PPE (as required):

  • Fall Protection Harness (>6 feet elevation)
  • Welding Hood & Protective Gear
  • Hearing Protection (>85 dB areas)
  • Respirators (confined spaces/fumes)

Hazard Recognition

Common Hazards in Steel Work:

  • Struck-By: Falling objects, moving equipment
  • Caught-In/Between: Pinch points, rotating equipment
  • Falls: Elevated work, openings, scaffolds
  • Electrocution: Power tools, welding equipment
  • Burns: Hot work, welding, cutting
  • Musculoskeletal: Heavy lifting, repetitive motion

Hazard Controls:

  1. Elimination/Substitution
  2. Engineering Controls
  3. Administrative Controls
  4. PPE (Last resort)

Emergency Procedures

Emergency Response:

  • Fire: Pull alarm, evacuate, call 911
  • Medical Emergency: Don't move injured, call for help
  • Severe Weather: Move to designated shelter area
  • Chemical Spill: Evacuate area, notify supervisor

Assembly Points:

  • Main Facility: Front parking lot near Joliet St entrance
  • Welding Shop: East side parking area
  • Fabrication Bay: North fence line
  • Job Sites: As designated by Site Supervisor

Crane & Rigging Safety

Critical Rules:

  • Never walk under suspended loads
  • Maintain clear communication with crane operator
  • Inspect rigging before each use
  • Know load weights and capacities
  • Use proper hand signals
  • Establish exclusion zones

Rigging Inspection Points:

  • Check slings for cuts, tears, or damage
  • Verify shackle pins are secured
  • Ensure proper load angles (≤60°)
  • Confirm tag lines are attached

Equipment & Tools Training

Learn about the specialized equipment and tools you'll use in steel fabrication and welding

Welding Equipment

  • MIG Welders - Gas Metal Arc Welding systems
  • Stick Welding Machines - Shielded Metal Arc Welding
  • Stud Welding Machines - Arc and capacitor-discharge stud welding systems
  • Fume Extraction Systems - Ensure compliance and clean air in the shop
  • Welding PPE & Accessories - Helmets, gloves, clamps, leads, and consumables

Safety: Always wear proper PPE and ensure adequate ventilation

Cutting Tools

  • Band Saws - Horizontal and vertical cutting
  • Plasma Cutters - High-precision cutting systems
  • Oxy-Acetylene Torches - Gas cutting and heating
  • Plasma CNC Tables - Heavy-duty precision cutting of plate, tube, and structural steel
  • Tube & Pipe CNC Cutters - Specialized systems for handrail, piping, and structural members
  • Angle Grinders - Portable cutting and grinding

Safety: Eye protection and proper guards required at all times

Lifting Equipment

  • Overhead Cranes - Bridge and gantry systems
  • Forklifts - Material handling vehicles
  • Chain Hoists - Manual and electric lifting
  • Come-Alongs - Portable winching systems
  • Rigging Hardware - Shackles, slings, chokers, and spreader bars for safe lifts
  • Beam Clamps & Trolleys - Quick rigging solutions for I-beam lifting and movement

Safety: Never exceed weight limits, inspect rigging before use

Measuring & Layout Tools

Precision instruments for shop fabrication and field erection

  • Tape Measures - Standard, metric, and dual-scale for structural layouts
  • Squares & Levels - Framing squares, combination squares, and digital levels for accuracy in fit-up
  • Calipers & Micrometers - Digital and dial precision tools for tight-tolerance measurements
  • Layout Tools - Scribes, center punches, soapstone, and chalk lines for marking steel
  • Plumb Bobs & Laser Levels - Ensuring vertical alignment during erection
  • Angle Finders & Protractors - For accurate cuts and weld prep on sloping/compound members
  • Total Stations - Robotic and manual instruments for high-precision layout of anchor bolts, columns, and grids
  • Theodolites - Optical instruments for measuring angles in horizontal and vertical planes
Quality: Accurate measurements ensure structural integrity

Fabrication Machinery

Heavy-duty equipment for precision forming, cutting, and automated production

  • Press Brake - 13' hydraulic forming system for bending plate and sheet up to ½"
  • Shear - 13' capacity shear for cutting plate and flat stock
  • Automated Welding Systems - Beam welders and robotic setups for high-production runs
  • Drill Line - CNC structural drilling system for beams and columns
  • Angle Line - CNC processing line for angles, flats, and clip connections
  • Heavy Equipment - Beam welders, copers, and shop cranes for large-scale assemblies
Safety: Authorized personnel only - proper training and certification required

Field Equipment

Specialized machinery for steel erection, lifting, and site support

  • 75-Ton Grove Crane - Hydraulic mobile crane for heavy lifts and long reaches
  • 30-Ton Crane - Versatile lifting solution for mid-sized structural steel picks
  • Spider Crane - Compact crane for confined-access erection and interior work
  • All-Terrain Forklifts - Rough-terrain material handling across job sites
  • Scissor Lifts - Aerial work platforms for welding, bolting, and installation at height
Safety: Certified operators only - proper licensing and inspection required
